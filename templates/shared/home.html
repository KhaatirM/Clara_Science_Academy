Latest improvements and enhancements · Last updated May 1, 2026

Latest System Updates – May 1, 2026

May 1 focuses on Google Workspace Directory automation (OUs, school-wide and per-class Groups), staff portal and welcome-email options, and clearer “school email” wording across the app. April 29 still covers dashboard zoom, quiz grading, and admin Student & Staff tables; older sections below document grading safeguards, rosters, report cards, and the full changelog.

Google Workspace, Staff Portal & Class Email Lists – May 1, 2026
  • Directory as single source of truth: A service account with domain-wide delegation can create Workspace users, move them between OUs, and sync Google Group membership from the database—so Admin Console stays aligned with the website roster.
  • Student OUs (including cohorts): Active students are organized under /Students by school level and Class of YYYY as an organizational unit path. Alumni and removal paths are supported for lifecycle and suspension rules. Graduation cohorts are not maintained as Google Groups mailing lists (Class of YYYY@); use school-level lists, studentassembly@, or per-class groups for mail. Legacy cohort groups in Admin may need a one-time cleanup.
  • School-wide student groups: Each eligible active student is kept in exactly one level group (elementary@, middle_school@, or highschool@) plus studentassembly@. Grade K–2 records follow the existing policy (no Workspace email until third grade).
  • Per-class Google Groups: Each class can have a stable list address such as class-12-2526@clarascienceacademy.org (internal class id plus a four-digit school-year key from the active year) and a human-readable group name matching the class title. Membership includes enrolled students (with school email) plus primary, additional, and substitute teachers. The current primary teacher is the group owner; when the primary changes, sync promotes the new primary before demoting the former one so someone always has owner rights.
  • Staff OUs and teachers@: Staff accounts are placed under /Staff (including paths for inactive or removed staff where policy applies). While a staff member is in an active OU, sync keeps them in teachers@ so faculty-wide email lists stay accurate.
  • Portal login vs directory-only: When adding or editing staff, leadership can turn website portal login on or off. If portal login is off, the person can still get a Google account and appear in the directory for email and Classroom, but they do not sign in to this website. The role dashboard reflects that distinction.
  • Welcome email to personal address: New staff can receive credentials at their personal email (no-reply sender). The message clearly separates website username and temporary portal password from Google / school email sign-in and labels the Workspace address as official school email (not generic “Workspace” jargon).
  • Multiple staff roles: Accounts can have a primary role plus additional roles stored on the user record. After sign-in, staff with more than one dashboard destination may use the Choose dashboard flow to pick where they land (and switch later from the sidebar).
  • Operations: Directory and class-group sync can be run on a schedule (cron). Ensure mail settings (for example MAIL_PASSWORD and public site URL) are configured if you rely on welcome messages. A one-time backfill script can create missing class groups where needed.

Impact: Google accounts, OUs, and group membership stay consistent with the website; teachers get reliable class mailing lists with correct ownership; staff understand portal vs Google sign-in; and cohort organization stays clear (OU-based, not duplicate cohort Groups).

Dashboard Zoom, Quiz Grading & Admin Tables – April 29, 2026
  • Sidebar and zoom: The dashboard keeps a fixed sidebar aligned with main content using an in-flow spacer and width sync on window resize and Visual Viewport changes (browser zoom). The slide-out drawer is used primarily below about 576px width so laptops and tablets at zoom keep the persistent rail; nav labels wrap more reliably in narrow rails.
  • Quiz grading: All quiz assignments open in the unified quiz grading view. The total score reflects auto-graded points plus manual short answer/essay scores. Grade links from the submissions list jump to the correct student card, with improved scroll positioning.
  • Student quiz feedback: Auto-graded score summaries are stored separately from teacher feedback, so students do not see system-generated text presented as a teacher comment.
  • Quiz attempts and reopen: When students use all allowed attempts, the quiz moves to inactive for them. Granting additional attempts via reopen updates both the student dashboard and the quiz details view so retakes work as expected.
  • Student Management (leadership): Streamlined statistics and a compact account status legend; Academic Status badges use high-contrast colors for readability; GPA correctly shows 0.00 when that is the stored value (instead of hiding it as “No Data”). Column headers and cell content are centered for easier scanning.
  • Teachers & Staff (leadership): The Staff Records table and integrated directory roster use the same centered layout for headers and columns, including actions.

Impact: Dashboards stay usable on scaled displays and zoomed browsers; quiz grading and student-facing quiz data are clearer and more trustworthy; school administrators get cleaner student and staff lists that are easier to read at a glance.

Grading Safeguards, Audit Logs & Staff Records – April 26, 2026
  • PDF/Paper grading: Teachers and admins can enter scores (including 0 for no submission) without first setting Submission Status to Online or In-Person. Submission status remains optional for tracking who turned work in.
  • Mixed-question quizzes — Grade Pending until finalized: Quizzes with essay/short answer questions now show auto-graded points immediately, but display Grade Pending to students until a teacher saves the manual scores (then the quiz becomes final).
  • Teacher + management grading consistency: Teacher and administrator grading share the same flows, and per-question quiz grading avoids creating grades for students who did not submit.
  • Grade spread accuracy: Grade distribution calculations properly account for voided records so charts and “not entered” counts are not misleading.
  • Grading Help updated + shown by default: The grading help popup was expanded with clear rules (0 vs blank, auto-save behavior, quiz pending/final) and appears automatically unless the user disables it.
  • Discussion assignment back button fixed: Back now returns to the correct class context instead of the generic Assignments & Grades landing view.
  • Teacher removal protection: When a teacher/staff member is removed, their assigned classes are reassigned to a placeholder so schedules remain intact, while historical records (created assignments, grades, markings) stay linked.
  • Audit logging — Tech only: All management access/actions are logged in a dedicated audit log. Viewing/exporting audit logs is restricted to Tech / IT Support (removed from School Admin+).
  • Printable staff activity record: Administrators can generate and export a printable record of a staff member’s assignments, attendance actions, submissions markings, and group grades.
  • Academic Alert popup behavior: Academic Alerts still appear across dashboards, but won’t pop up while you’re actively grading an assignment to keep the grading screen uncluttered.

Impact: Grading is safer and harder to do “by accident,” students get clearer quiz status, and leadership/tech teams have stronger visibility into actions and staff activity without widening access.

Staff & Quiz Builder – April 23, 2026
  • Teachers & Staff – current + former roster: A roster view is now built into the Teachers & Staff area for Directors and School Administrators. You can switch between current employees and former employees (inactive/removed) and search by name, email, role, department, or staff ID.
  • Cleaner navigation: The separate Staff Directory tab/link was removed to avoid redundancy; everything lives under Teachers & Staff. Existing staff-directory links continue to work via an automatic redirect.
  • Quiz creation – drag and drop like Google Forms: Sections and questions can now be reordered by click-and-drag using a grip handle. Sections move as a group with their questions.
  • Quiz creation – move + delete fixed: Moving a question to a different section now works reliably, section membership is clearly shown, and deleting sections/questions works correctly.

Impact: Admins can quickly find both active and historical employee records without switching pages. Quiz creation is faster and less error-prone when building multi-section quizzes.

Staff Assignment Labels & Dashboard Sidebar – April 18, 2026
  • Teacher and administrator assignment views: “Overdue” and “Past Due” style badges were removed from staff-facing assignment lists and headers. Status is shown with neutral, lifecycle-aware wording (for example active, inactive, upcoming, or voided) instead of framing work as overdue for the people who assigned it.
  • School leadership dashboards: Management dashboard cards and tables use the same idea—calendar and due-date context without an overdue badge directed at staff. Stat labels were adjusted so counts are not labeled as overdue for administrators.
  • Academic Concerns & related tools: Summary and filter wording in the academic concerns experience was updated (for example “After calendar due date” and “Behind on due dates only”) so teachers and admins see clear language without an overdue badge. Similar wording updates apply to deadline reminders for teachers and the redo dashboard for administrators (past-deadline phrasing instead of “Overdue”).
  • Group assignment (admin) time note: When the due moment has passed, the secondary note describes how long ago the due date was, without calling the assignment overdue for staff.
  • Dashboard sidebar on small and scaled screens: Below about 576px width (typical phones in portrait), the sidebar opens as a full-width slide-out drawer. Wider viewports—including Surfaces and other laptops at browser zoom—keep the persistent rail; main content uses top padding in drawer mode so it clears the fixed menu control.

Impact: Staff see assignment and alert language that fits their role, and teachers and administrators can reach every sidebar destination on compact or scaled screens without hunting for icons or sideways scrolling.

Report Cards, Grading Policy & Safeguards – April 5, 2026
  • Updated grading policy (Q4 onward): Letter-grade cutoffs, +/- handling, GPA point mapping, and grade distribution were updated system-wide. Failing letter output now uses E instead of legacy failing labels in all key grading/report-card paths.
  • Report-card confirmation now profile-synced: Student confirmation data in report-card generation is now sourced from student records to improve accuracy. Required profile fields for this flow include Gender and Entrance School Year (with school-year choices going back to 2020).
  • Generation alerts for leadership: Whenever a report card is generated, Directors and School Administrators now receive in-app and email alerts. The system also checks quarter-grade continuity and flags inconsistent patterns for review.
  • Templates and outputs aligned: Report-card templates and sanitization logic were refreshed so generated PDFs and viewed report-card data remain consistent with the current grading model.

Impact: Grading and report cards are now aligned under one policy, generation is more auditable, and school leadership gets immediate visibility when report cards are created or need review.

Assignments & Group Assignment Experience – April 4, 2026
  • Teacher grading workflow cleanup: Grade-distribution visuals and filters were redesigned, ungraded logic was corrected (including valid 0 scores), and grading actions now better respect voided records.
  • Assignment view/back navigation refinements: Assignment view and grading pages were reorganized for clarity, with back-navigation behavior tuned to return users to the right class-scoped assignments view.
  • Group assignment view upgrades: Document/PDF display, compact details, submissions modal, and void/unvoid controls were improved for both teachers and administrators, with refreshed modal/button styling for consistency.
  • Group assignment creation/edit improvements: File-upload UI for PDF/Paper flows was redesigned, and group-assignment editing now supports replacing uploaded documents.

Impact: Assignment workflows are cleaner and faster to use, document handling is more reliable, and group-assignment management is clearer across creation, viewing, and grading.

Student Assistant, Attendance & Status Consistency – April 3, 2026
  • Student Assistant expansion: Assistants can now propose quiz and discussion assignments (in addition to PDF/Paper) through the established approval workflow.
  • Attendance simplification: School-day attendance controls were streamlined by consolidating overlapping absence actions while preserving the same underlying behavior.
  • Voided status shown correctly to students: Student class/recent-assignment views now correctly surface Voided instead of misleading late/not-submitted states where applicable.
  • Selector clarity for group assignment types: Group Discussion remains visible but marked Under Development and non-selectable, keeping the available path clear.

Impact: Assistant capabilities are broader, attendance actions are easier to use, and students get more accurate status visibility across assignments.

Teacher Home, GPA Timing & Period Reminders – March 27, 2026
  • Assignments & Grades in one place: The teacher dashboard no longer includes a separate Grades quick action that opened a redundant list. Assignments & Grades is the hub for viewing classes and grading; the old /teacher/grades link now redirects there automatically.
  • Quarter & semester GPA — “Calculating” window: After each period’s end date at 4:00 PM Eastern, the student Grades view shows Calculating for up to seven days while teachers finalize marks—instead of jumping straight to N/A. After that window, the usual percentage and GPA display for the period.
  • Automatic period reminders: On the day that is two weeks before each active quarter or semester end date (using the school’s configured calendar), the system sends in-app notifications and emails: students are reminded to turn in outstanding work; teachers, school administrators, and directors are reminded to finalize grades. Each period and audience is notified once. Production sites should run the daily reminder job (or the secured HTTP cron endpoint) and may set PUBLIC_BASE_URL so email links open the full site.

Impact: Teachers see a simpler home screen; families understand when official period GPA is still being finalized; and the whole school gets a timely nudge before grading periods close.

Student Assistant Proposals & Approvals – March 26, 2026
  • Teacher approval before students see work: When a student assistant creates an individual or group assignment, it stays hidden from the class until a teacher or school administrator approves it. Rejected proposals do not appear to students. The assistant hub explains that approval is required.
  • Where to find pending proposals: Assignments & Grades includes an Assistant proposals control and alert-style banners when something is waiting; selected class and class cards show counts and shortcuts. View class and the class roster also surface alerts and links so teachers do not miss proposals while teaching or reviewing a roster.
  • Review, approve, or reject: A dedicated pending assistant assignments page lists each proposal with context; teachers can approve or reject (with confirmation where appropriate). After approval, the assignment follows your normal visibility rules for the class.
  • Clearer, consistent UI: Buttons, badges, and alert strips for this flow use the same indigo/violet accent language as the rest of the dashboard—gradient primary actions, readable badges for pending counts, and a styled hub card for “create assignment” actions so the experience does not feel generic.

Impact: Assistants can propose work safely; teachers stay in control of what the class sees; pending work is visible from the places staff already use; and the approval experience looks intentional.

Academic Alerts, Reopen & Void, Submissions, Student Assistants – March 25, 2026
  • Academic concerns – submission clarity: Alert cards now include a Submitted / Not submitted badge that matches how grading works in the app: if a teacher entered a grade (including in-person paper work), it counts as submitted even when no online upload exists. The detailed student view uses the same rule. Alerts ignore voided assignments and voided group grades so they do not inflate concern lists.
  • Reopen & voided grades (including quizzes): Granting a reopen or extra quiz attempts does not apply while a student’s grade for that assignment is voided. Those students are skipped with a clear message; the reopen list shows a warning and disables their row. Learners cannot open a voided quiz until the grade is restored (unvoid). Submission access treats voided grades as closed.
  • View Submissions (teachers & admins): The submissions page for an assignment now shows when a student’s grade is voided (badge and explanation), including a note that reopening will not help until the grade is restored.
  • Student assistant – multiple classes: Assistants who cover two classes get an Assistant console at /assistant/, a refreshed dashboard promo with per-class shortcuts, and an in-page class switcher on the workspace, attendance, and grading screens so moving between classes is one click.

Impact: Fewer mixed signals between grades and “submitted” in alerts; reopen and quiz retakes stay honest when work is voided; staff see void status on submissions; assistants save time when they support more than one class.

Tech – Student Devices & Bulk CSV – March 24, 2026
  • Devices tab (Tech / IT): Track school-issued laptops for 3rd grade and above and tablets for 2nd grade and below (kindergarten counts as grade “K”). Each record includes an inventory/asset name (for example CSA-Laptop-# or CSA-Tablet-#), device name, cord number (often the same as the device number), operating system, and one assigned student per device.
  • Bulk upload: From the Devices page, download a CSV template, fill in rows, and Import. Rows are matched to students by internal ID (student_db_id) or school student ID. Existing asset_name values are updated; new rows create assignments. Imports run per row so one bad line does not discard the rest.

Impact: IT can maintain a clear inventory of who has which laptop or tablet and load or refresh data from a spreadsheet.

Student Assistants – Eligibility & Limits – March 24, 2026
  • Who can be an assistant: Directors and School Administrators choose assistants when editing a class. A student qualifies if they are enrolled in that class, or if they are not in the class but their grade is at or above the lowest grade set for that class (when grade levels are configured). The picker lists students with any enrollment in the same school year. If the class has no grade levels, only enrolled students may be assistants.
  • Limits: At most two student assistants per class. Each student may assist at most two classes at a time (other assignments count toward that cap when saving).

Impact: Assistants can be chosen flexibly from the right grades (or from the roster) while keeping workloads fair and capped.

Group Assignments – Void, Grading & Submissions – March 19, 2026
  • Void group assignments (teachers & admins): When viewing a group assignment in Assignments & Grades, teachers and school administrators can now void assignments for specific groups or individual students—not just for the entire class. The void/unvoid controls are available from the group assignment view page, so you can exclude particular groups or students from grading without affecting others.
  • Blank score = 0 in group grading: When grading group assignments, leaving a student's score blank now correctly saves as 0 and is included in grade calculations. Previously, blank scores could be skipped; now all students in the group receive a grade (including 0 when appropriate).
  • Submission count on assignment cards: Assignment cards now display the correct submission count. When teachers mark students as "Submit in person" or "Online," those submissions are counted. The count reflects both file uploads and manually marked in-person submissions.
  • Submission notes dropdown: Submission notes have been replaced with a dropdown on individual and group assignment grading pages: On-Time, Late, or Other (which reveals a text field for custom notes). This applies to teacher grading, admin grading, and student assistant grading for both individual and group assignments.

Impact: Teachers and admins have fine-grained control over group assignment voiding. Grading is more accurate with blank scores handled correctly. Submission counts reflect real work turned in. Submission notes are faster to enter with consistent options.

Academic Concerns – Submission Status Badge – March 19, 2026
  • Submission status badge: Each assignment in the Academic Concerns popup (the modal that shows when students have missing or failing work) now displays a Submitted or Not Submitted badge. At a glance, you can see whether the student turned in the assignment (online or in person) before it was graded—helping you distinguish between "didn't submit" and "submitted but failed."
  • Printout includes submission status: When printing a student's academic concern report, the submission status (Submitted or Not Submitted) is included for each assignment alongside the status and due date.

Impact: Teachers and administrators can quickly see which at-risk students have turned in their work versus those who haven't, making follow-up and interventions more targeted.

Academic Concerns & Student Assignments – March 18, 2026
  • Academic concerns on every tab: The Academic Alert popup (the corner notification about students with academic concerns) now appears on every tab for teachers and school administrators—not just the Home tab. Whether you're on My Classes, Assignments & Grades, Students, or any other page, you'll see the alert if there are students who need attention. Click "View Details" to open the full modal with student cards.
  • Academic concerns – custom point values: The academic concerns logic now correctly handles assignments where teachers use custom total points (e.g. 25, 50, or 150 instead of 100). Previously, a score like 17/25 (68%) could be misidentified because the system assumed 100-point scale. The alert now uses percentage for the failing threshold (≤69%), so 17/25 correctly flags as failing while 20/25 (80%) does not.
  • Student assignment cards – creator display: Each assignment card on the student Assignments tab now shows the teacher or school administrator who created it, matching the display on the teacher/admin side. Students see the creator's name (or username) directly on the card, so they know who assigned each task. Applies to both individual and group assignments in Upcoming, Active, and Inactive sections.
  • Academic alerts search and filters: The search and filter toolbar in the Academic Concerns modal has been restored. Teachers and administrators can search by student name, filter by type (All, Failing, Missing, after calendar due date), filter by GPA (All, Critical <2.0, Warning 2.0–2.9, Below 3.0), sort by name/GPA/assignments count, and reset filters—making it easier to focus on the students who need the most attention.
  • Assignment document 404 on Render – fix documented: Assignment PDFs and documents that return 404 after each deploy on Render (due to the ephemeral filesystem) can now be fixed by using a Persistent Disk. Documentation has been added (docs/RENDER_UPLOAD_404_FIX.md) explaining the cause, how to configure a Persistent Disk and mount /static/uploads, and optional cloud storage (S3) alternatives. render.yaml comments were updated with disk config examples.

Impact: Teachers and administrators stay aware of academic concerns no matter which tab they're on, with search and filters to quickly find specific students. Academic alerts are accurate for all grading scales. Students can see who assigned each piece of work. Admins deploying to Render have clear guidance on avoiding assignment document 404s.

Assignment Documents & Redo Dashboard – March 17, 2026
  • Assignment document 404 fix: Assignments with uploaded documents (PDFs, etc.) that previously returned 404 errors when viewing or downloading now load correctly. Path resolution was improved to handle relative paths and fallback locations, so files are found even after deploys or when the app runs on different servers. New uploads store portable paths for reliable access.
  • Redo Dashboard notification badge: The Redo Dashboard button in Assignments & Grades now shows a notification badge (like Extension Requests) with the count of pending redo requests from students. Teachers see counts for their classes; Directors and School Administrators see all pending requests.

Impact: Assignment documents load reliably for students and teachers. Teachers and administrators can quickly see how many redo requests are waiting for review without opening the Redo Dashboard first.

Assignment Badges, Reopen Logic & Void/Edit Fixes – March 17, 2026
  • Two distinct assignment badges: Assignment cards now show two separate badges: Context (In-Class vs Homework) and Category (Tests, Projects, Quizzes, Labs, Participation, Other). The Category badge correlates to the category question in the PDF/Paper assignment creation form and is used for weighted grading.
  • Category badge for group assignments: Group assignments now always display the assignment category badge in card view, table view, and grades view—consistent with individual PDF/Paper assignments.
  • PDF/Paper edit form – Category question: The edit form for PDF/Paper assignments includes the Category and Category Weight fields. Both teacher and management edit routes correctly save assignment category when updating an assignment.
  • Reopen logic for PDF/Paper: Reopening now works correctly for all scenarios. For Upcoming assignments, a reopening grants early access before the open date. For Inactive assignments, students with valid extensions can still submit without needing a reopening; reopenings and redos continue to work for those past their window.
  • Reopen status – accurate for PDF/Paper: The Reopen modal’s student list now uses the same logic as submission access. Students who can submit (including via extensions) no longer appear as "needs reopening." Only students who truly cannot submit are flagged.
  • Void modal – UI fix: When selecting "Void for Selected Students Only," that option is now correctly highlighted (blue) instead of still showing "Void for Entire Class" as selected. The active choice matches the student selection section.
  • Duplicate Edit button removed: The assignment view page had two Edit buttons. The duplicate has been removed. Quiz assignments link to the quiz editor; PDF/Paper and discussion link to the appropriate edit form.
  • Void form for School Administrators: When voiding from the assignment view page, the form now correctly voids for the entire class when "Void for Entire Class" is selected. After voiding, School Administrators are redirected back to the assignment view instead of the assignments list.

Impact: Clearer assignment categorization for grading, reliable reopen and extension behavior for PDF/Paper, accurate void/unvoid/extension flows, and a cleaner assignment view with no duplicate Edit button.

10 New Themes – March 10, 2026
  • New themes in Settings: Students, teachers, and staff can now choose from 10 additional themes: Sunset (warm orange & coral), Midnight (deep purple & indigo), Desert (sand & terracotta), Lavender (soft purples), Rose (blush pink), Cherry Blossom (sakura pink), Aurora (northern lights teals & greens), Storm (slate grey & storm blue), Wine (burgundy & deep red), and Mint (soft mint green).
  • Where to find them: Go to Settings (Students: Settings tab; Teachers/Admins: gear icon) and open the Theme dropdown. The new themes appear under the "Color" section alongside Ocean and Forest.
  • Tech site override: Tech users can also set any of these themes site-wide from the System page, so everyone sees the same theme (e.g. Mint for St. Patrick's Day, Lavender for spring).

Impact: 20 total themes now available—personalize your dashboard with a look that works for you.

Discussion Assignments – File Uploads – March 10, 2026
  • Upload images and documents: Students can now attach files when creating discussion threads or posting replies. Supported formats include images (PNG, JPG, GIF, WebP) and documents (PDF, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, text, Markdown).
